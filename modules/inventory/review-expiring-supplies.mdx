Use **Expiring Supplies** to see supply lots that have expired or will expire within the next 30 days, and act on them from one view.

## Prerequisites

- Access to **Inventory > Supplies**.
- Supply items tracked by lot batch and expiration date. See [Add supplies or equipment](/modules/inventory/add-supplies-or-equipment).

## Open the page

Go to **Inventory > Expiring Supplies** in the Inventory sidebar.

The page groups lots into two lists:

- **Expired**: lots past their expiration date that should be pulled or reviewed before use.
- **Expiring Soon**: lots that will expire within 30 days.

Each list shows the supply, location, lot number, expiration date, quantity on hand, and days remaining or days overdue. Only supply lots with an expiration date are included.

## Metrics at the top

- **Expired Lots**: total lots past expiration.
- **Expiring Soon**: total lots expiring within 30 days.
- **Affected Supplies**: distinct supply items at risk.
- **Units at Risk**: total quantity across all affected lots.

## Act on a lot

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the lot">
    Search by supply name, SKU, lot number, or location. Lots are sorted by soonest expiration first.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the supply">
    Select the row or the external-link icon to open the supply record and see all of its lots and locations.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Adjust stock">
    Select **Adjust** on the lot's row to open the stock adjustment dialog with the supply and location pre-filled. Remove expired quantity, transfer stock, or record a waste reason.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Common problems

### A lot I expect to see is missing

Only lots with an expiration date and a **Supply** category appear. Confirm the item is categorized as a supply and that the lot has an expiration date on file.

### Expired stock is still showing on hand

Use **Adjust** to remove the expired quantity from the lot. The expired lot will drop off the list once its quantity on hand is zero.

### I need a longer window than 30 days

The page shows the next 30 days. For longer-range planning, open the supply record to see every lot and expiration date.
