Use **Schedule** to create shifts and publish them when they are ready for crews.

## Before you start

Make sure the needed stations, units, shift templates, and personnel records are set up. If your agency uses rotation patterns, confirm the rotation setup before generating draft shifts.

## Steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Schedule">
    Go to **Schedule**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose the schedule view">
    Pick the date range and view that match the schedule you are building.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create or generate shifts">
    Add shifts manually, use shift templates, or use the rotation builder if your agency schedules by rotation.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Assign personnel">
    Assign people to the shift roles that need coverage.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review warnings">
    Check coverage gaps, minimum staffing warnings, fatigue signals, and compliance warnings before publishing.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Publish the schedule">
    Publish draft shifts when the schedule is ready for crews to see.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What LifelineIQ changes

Draft shifts stay hidden from crews until they are published. Published shifts become available to schedule views, crew workflows, and schedule-related review tools.

## Common problems

### I cannot publish the schedule

Review staffing and compliance warnings. LifelineIQ can block publishing when required staffing rules are not met.

### A person cannot be assigned

Check whether they are already assigned, unavailable, missing required credentials, or blocked by a scheduling rule.
