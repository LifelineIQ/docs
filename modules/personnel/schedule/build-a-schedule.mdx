Use **Provider \> Schedule** to build and manage agency staffing. The schedule page lets schedulers work by calendar, open shifts, open roles, divisions, stations, crews, rotations, and saved views.

<Frame caption="Schedule calendar view in the demo workspace.">
  ![Schedule calendar showing month view, filters, open roles, grouping controls, KPI warnings, and Add Shift.](/images/screenshots/schedule-calendar.png)
</Frame>

## Before you start

Make sure the people, stations, divisions, crews, rotations, and scheduling rules your agency uses have already been configured.

You need scheduler or administrator access for the active agency.

## What this page is for

Use Schedule when you need to create coverage for a date, fill open roles, monitor warnings, approve schedule-related requests, and make sure crews can see the final schedule.

## Steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Schedule">
    Go to **Provider** and select **Schedule** under **Scheduling**. The current route is `/schedule`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick the schedule view">
    Use the view controls at the top of the page. Common controls include **Calendar**, **Open shifts**, the date arrows, **Month - Calendar**, **Open roles**, **Group by Division**, and **Views**.

    Choose the view that matches the job you are doing. Calendar view is useful for building a schedule by date. Open shifts and open roles are useful when you are trying to close gaps.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Filter to the right coverage area">
    Use **All divisions**, **All stations**, **All crews**, and **All rotations** to narrow the schedule before adding shifts.

    Filtering first helps prevent creating a shift under the wrong station, crew, or rotation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the warning chips">
    Check the chips above the calendar, such as open-role coverage warnings, **Paging**, **Alerts**, **Requests**, and **Signups**.

    A red open-role warning means LifelineIQ sees required coverage that is not staffed yet.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a shift">
    Select **Add Shift**. Enter the shift details required by your agency, such as date, time, assignment location, role, crew, unit, or other staffing fields shown in the drawer.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign personnel">
    Add the right person to each role. Watch for conflicts, unavailable personnel, missing credentials, fatigue warnings, or staffing-rule warnings before saving.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill open roles">
    Use **Open roles** when you need to see what still needs coverage. Work each gap until the warning count is resolved or intentionally left open according to agency policy.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review requests and signups">
    Use the **Requests** and **Signups** indicators when staff have requested time off, trades, open shifts, or other schedule changes. Resolve those items before treating the schedule as final.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save or publish based on your workflow">
    Save draft work while you are still building. Publish or release the schedule only when coverage, warnings, and request decisions are ready for staff to see.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What good looks like

A complete schedule has the correct date range, station/division/crew context, all required roles covered or intentionally marked as open, and no unresolved warnings that should block release.

## Common problems

### The schedule shows open roles

Open **Open roles** and review each gap. Add a person, create an open shift, page/call out if your agency uses that workflow, or document why the role remains open.

### I cannot assign a person

Check for availability, overlapping shifts, credential requirements, employment status, fatigue rules, or whether the person belongs to the division/station/crew you are scheduling.

### Staff cannot see the schedule

Confirm the schedule has been published or released according to your agency workflow. Draft shifts may not appear in staff-facing Relay views.

### I built the shift in the wrong place

Open the shift, correct the station/division/crew/role if allowed, or remove the incorrect shift and rebuild it in the correct schedule context.
