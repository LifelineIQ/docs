Use Safety reporting when something happened, almost happened, or needs follow-up to protect people, patients, equipment, or operations.

## Steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Safety incidents">
    Go to **Safety > Incidents**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose the report type">
    Select **Field Report**, **Report Incident**, or **Near Miss**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter incident details">
    Choose the report type, category, priority, unit or location, title, description, date, time, and location.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete field triage">
    Record the immediate action taken and any follow-up request.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add people details">
    Add involved employees or an assignee when known.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete safety details">
    Add injury, exposure, vehicle, equipment, or OSHA information when it applies.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit the report">
    Select **Report Incident** or **Report Near-Miss**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What happens next

The incident appears in **Safety > Incidents**. Safety leaders can open it, review the investigation, add corrective actions, manage actions and resolution, and close the incident when follow-up is complete.

## Common problems

### I am not sure whether this is a near miss

Use **Near Miss** when harm did not occur but could have. A safety leader can reclassify or add details later.

### I need someone to contact me

Use the follow-up fields and include the best contact route when allowed.

### I need to continue an investigation

Open the incident from **Safety > Incidents**, then use the **Safety Investigation**, **Corrective Actions**, or **Actions & Resolution** tabs.
