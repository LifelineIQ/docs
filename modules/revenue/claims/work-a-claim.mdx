Use **Revenue \> Prepare Claims** when a claim needs review before billing. This worklist is where billing users search, group, filter, import, create, and queue claims that are not ready to submit yet.

<Frame caption="Revenue claims worklist in the demo workspace. No patient or claim details are shown.">
  ![Revenue Prepare Claims worklist showing queue filters, search, grouping controls, and claim action buttons.](/images/screenshots/revenue-claims.png)
</Frame>

## Before you start

You need access to the **Revenue** module for the active agency.

Open **Revenue** and select **Prepare Claims** under **Claims by Stage**. The current route is `/revenue/claims`.

## What this page is for

Use this page to answer four questions before a claim goes out:

- Is the claim in the right queue?
- Is required patient, run, coverage, service, coding, document, and charge information present?
- Is there a validation or follow-up issue that must be fixed first?
- Is the claim ready for submission, or should it move to payer follow-up, denial work, collections, or another queue?

## Steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the claims worklist">
    Go to **Revenue** and select **Prepare Claims**. Confirm that you are in the correct agency at the top of LifelineIQ.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose the queue you want to work">
    Use the queue chips across the top of the worklist, such as **Ready to Submit**, **Denied**, **Timely Filing Risk**, **Follow-Up Due**, and **Unassigned**.

    Choose **My Claims**, **Unassigned**, or **All** to control whose claims appear.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the claim">
    Use **Search claims...** when you know the claim number, run number, patient name, or another searchable value.

    Use the **All Claims** dropdown, **Tags**, **Filters**, **Group by**, and **Columns** controls when you need to narrow a large queue.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the claim record">
    Select the claim row. Review the visible worklist columns first: **Status**, **Claim #**, **Run #**, **Patient Name**, **Service Date**, **Patient**, **Coverage**, **Service**, **Coding**, **Docs**, **Waystar**, **Charge**, and **Tags**.

    These columns tell you which part of the claim needs attention before you open the full record.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fix the blocking item">
    Work the missing or flagged section before moving the claim forward. Common examples are missing coverage, incomplete patient information, coding issues, missing documents, charge problems, or Waystar/submission readiness problems.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use intake readiness when no claims are listed">
    If the page shows **No claims yet**, select **Review intake readiness**. That sends you to the handoff review area where imported incidents, completed charts, or pre-billing items can be checked before they become claim work.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Move the claim to the next correct queue">
    After the blocking item is resolved, route the claim based on its next action. Claims that are clean can move toward submission. Claims with payer problems, denials, collection needs, or other unresolved work should move to the matching queue instead of being forced through submission.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What good looks like

A ready claim has no unresolved patient, coverage, service, coding, document, charge, or submission readiness blockers. The claim is in the correct queue, assigned if your team uses assignment, and has enough history for the next biller to understand what changed.

## Common problems

### I cannot find the claim

Clear filters, switch from **My Claims** to **All**, and check other claim stages such as **Payer Follow-Up**, **Collections**, **Closed Claims**, and the submission queue.

### The worklist is empty

Use **Review intake readiness**. Claims may not exist yet if the upstream chart, incident, import, or pre-billing handoff has not been completed.

### I do not know what to fix first

Start with the worklist columns. A missing or failed value in **Patient**, **Coverage**, **Service**, **Coding**, **Docs**, **Waystar**, or **Charge** usually points to the next section to open.

### The claim should not be submitted

Do not submit it just to clear the queue. Move it to the appropriate follow-up, denial, collections, or closed workflow and document the reason.
